One Sip

🍺
🎸1 SIP

A wind machine is used on stage.

🔥1 SIP

There is fire, pyrotechnics, or a firework effect on stage.

🧝1 SIP

A performer wears a costume that defies all logic.

💃1 SIP

There are backing dancers doing choreography that is… aggressively enthusiastic.

🎤1 SIP

Someone hits a completely unnecessary high note.

😢1 SIP

The song is performed entirely in a language no one in the room can identify.

🌈1 SIP

A rainbow flag or queer flag appears on screen or in the crowd.

🥁1 SIP

A commentator makes a subtle jab at a country's voting bloc politics.

🎻1 SIP

An instrument is played on stage that you would not expect at Eurovision (e.g. accordion, didgeridoo, kazoo).

💬1 SIP

A spokesperson says their country's name with palpable national pride.

Two Sips

🍻
🎭2 SIPS

A staging concept is so abstract that no one in the room can explain what it means.

💑2 SIPS

Two countries give each other twelve points and everyone groans.

🤝2 SIPS

A Nordic country votes for another Nordic country. Shocking scenes.

📞2 SIPS

A national spokesperson's connection cuts out mid-announcement.

😬2 SIPS

The UK entry gets zero from the jury. Pour one out.

🪄2 SIPS

Someone performs a "magic trick" or illusion as part of the staging.

🌍2 SIPS

A country uses their own language but throws in one random English word for no reason.

🐦2 SIPS

An animal or animal motif appears on stage (real or costume).

Three Sips

🥂
😭3 SIPS

Anyone in the room cries. They must drink as well.

🎉3 SIPS

Australia gets a suspiciously high score, triggering the annual "but ARE they even European?" debate.

🕊️3 SIPS

A wildcard country no one expected makes the top 5.

🎤3 SIPS

A notable wardrobe malfunction, prop failure, or stage invasion occurs.

📺3 SIPS

Graham Norton (or whoever's commentating) delivers a line so devastating it deserves its own BAFTA.

🇬🇧 UK Special Rules

⚠️ These Rules Apply to the UK Entry Only

  • When the UK entry performs: everyone must stand up, belt a lyric they don't know, and sit back down.
  • If the UK places in the Top 5: everyone cheers and takes a celebratory sip. No arguing allowed for 2 minutes.
  • If the UK places in the Bottom 5: last person to say "We wuz robbed!" finishes their drink.
  • If the UK gets nul points: full group shot. This is a national emergency. Handle it as such.
  • If the UK wins: everyone in the room calls one friend or family member not watching and screams down the phone. Then drinks.
  • Whenever any UK spokesperson appears on screen: mandatory sip of tea (or whatever you're having).

The Voting Gauntlet

📊
🗳️

When "And the twelve points go to…" is said: everyone freezes until the country is announced. First person to move drinks.

😤

If you correctly predicted a country's douze points before they gave it: nominate someone to drink two sips.

🤫

A spokesperson in a glamorous location makes everyone jealous: one collective sip of envy.

📵

If the leaderboard changes dramatically in the final votes: last person to gasp theatrically drinks.

💀

If the winning country is one you'd never heard of before tonight: everyone finishes their drink in solidarity.

🎯

Whoever guessed the winner at the start of the night: distributes five sips however they choose.

House Rules & Punishments

⚖️
📱

Phone Check: Anyone caught scrolling their phone during a performance drinks two sips. No excuses. Not even for "just checking the score".

🏆

Bold Prediction: At the start, everyone names their winner. Whoever is most wrong at the end must make everyone a round of drinks.

🙄

The Cynic Tax: Every time someone says "this is rubbish" or "they're all the same" — they drink. Keep cynicism expensive.

🇩🇪

Big Four Solidarity: Germany, Spain, France, or Italy get 12 points from a neighbour — everyone drinks to the mystery of geopolitics.

🎶

Earworm Penalty: If you catch yourself humming an entry during the adverts, confess and drink one sip. Denial is worse.

🧑‍⚖️

The Jury Is Out: If you disagree loudly with a jury score, the room votes — if majority agrees with you, you nominate two sips; if not, you drink them.

The Grand Finale

✨

🏆 When a Winner is Crowned 🏆

The person who picked the winner raises their glass. Everyone else toasts them. They are the Eurovision Oracle and must be consulted on all matters of camp taste for the remainder of the evening.

The person who placed last in predictions must do the washing up, explain why the UK's entry was actually robbed, and hum the winning song on request until midnight.
